# In-Domain vs Task-Aware Validation for SSL Pre-training Monitoring & Stopping

*Research-agent report, 2026-06-05. Web-verified against primary sources (papers + official repos). Verdicts: **[established]** / **[folklore]** / **[open]**. Motivated by the bias in our current loop: a kNN probe on **CIFAR-10** (the downstream domain) drives LR-anneal and early-stop, making pre-training task-aware. Fix direction: validate on the pre-training domain (Tiny-ImageNet, TI).*

## Q1 — How published SSL works actually monitor pre-training progress

The split each method probes on matters; below is what I verified.

- **DINO** (Caron et al. 2021, arXiv:2104.14294): kNN is the canonical *evaluation*, run via `eval_knn.py`. Verified defaults: `nb_knn default=[10,20,100,200]` ("20 is usually working the best"), `temperature=0.07`; database = ImageNet **train** split (`data_path/train`), queries = ImageNet **val**. README only documents it as a post-hoc eval step ("To evaluate a simple k-NN classifier ... run"); **online kNN-during-training is not in the official repo** — it is a community add-on. So DINO's probe is in-domain (IN train→val), full-train db. **[established for the eval protocol; online-during-training is folklore]**
- **DINOv2** (Oquab et al. 2023, arXiv:2304.07193): teacher weights dumped every 12,500 iters to an `eval` folder; kNN + linear eval run **separately, on ImageNet val**, after the fact. No online monitor in the training loop. **[established]**
- **MoCo-v3** (Chen et al. 2021, arXiv:2104.02057): diagnoses ViT instability using **linear-probe / kNN accuracy curves vs. training**, but their published instability analysis (the "dip" figures) is driven by *training curves* and IN-1k linear probe — in-domain. They do *not* peek at a different downstream domain for stopping (they don't early-stop at all; fixed 300 ep). **[established]**
- **solo-learn** (Turrisi et al., arXiv:2108.01775 — our reference repo): supports **online linear evaluation** (a linear head trained alongside SSL on the *same* pre-training dataset, reported as `online` top-1) and an offline `main_knn.py`. Online probe is in-domain by construction. **[established]**
- **SimSiam / BYOL / MoCo / SimCLR** (original repos): monitor the **SSL loss only**; kNN-monitor-during-training on the pre-training set (e.g. CIFAR train→test, k≈200) is a widely-copied *community* pattern (e.g. `gathierry/simsiam-with-knn-monitor`, lightly's `BenchmarkModule`), not in the original FAIR/DeepMind code. **[folklore, in-domain when used]**
- **Barlow Twins / VICReg** (Zbontar 2021 arXiv:2103.03230; Bardes 2021 arXiv:2105.04906): fixed 1000-ep budget, **no online probe, no early stop**; loss is the only training-time signal. **[established]**
- **MAE / iBOT** (He 2021 arXiv:2111.06377; Zhou 2021 arXiv:2111.07832): fixed budget; quality judged post-hoc by linear/kNN on IN val. iBOT reports 82.3% IN linear probe as an *outcome*, not an online monitor. **[established]**

**Synthesis:** the universal convention is *in-domain* probing (the pre-training dataset's own train/val), used for **evaluation**, not for stopping. **No major method early-stops on a probe at all**, and **none probe a different downstream domain** to make pre-training decisions. Our CIFAR-10-driven stop is unconventional on *two* axes (online-stopping + cross-domain). The cross-domain peek is the one that biases the data-efficiency claim and must be removed.

## Q2 — Label-free / embedding-statistics alternatives for selection & stopping

- **RankMe** (Garrido et al. 2022, arXiv:2210.02885, ICML'23): `RankMe(Z)=exp(−Σ pₖ log pₖ)`, pₖ = σₖ(Z)/‖σ(Z)‖₁+ε — Shannon entropy (smooth/effective rank) of embedding singular values, computed on **25,600 unlabeled pre-training samples** (10k already ≥95% accurate, App. G). Correlates with downstream acc, label-free, no HPs. Stated limits: *"should only be used to compare different runs of a given method"*; predictive power **plateaus once rank exceeds #classes** — a real concern for us (200 TI classes, 192-dim ViT-Tiny embeddings → rank ceiling near embed dim). **[established at IN scale]**
- **α-ReQ** (Agrawal et al., NeurIPS 2022): power-law decay coefficient α of the feature-covariance eigenspectrum; "well-defined intervals of α" track linear-readout quality. RankMe paper shows α-ReQ's power-law assumption **breaks under collapse**; RankMe is more robust. **[established; weaker than RankMe]**
- **LiDAR** (Thilak et al. 2023, arXiv:2312.04000): rank of the LDA matrix of the *surrogate SSL task*; explicitly fixes RankMe's failure to "discriminate informative vs uninformative features," outperforming naive covariance-rank for HP selection. Needs the SSL task structure (multiple views/clusters). **[established; better predictor than RankMe, but heavier]**
- **Alignment & Uniformity** (Wang & Isola 2020, arXiv:2005.10242): two optimizable metrics; strong agreement with downstream acc — but defined for the *contrastive/hypersphere* setting. Barlow Twins is redundancy-reduction, not L2-normalized contrastive, so direct transfer of the uniformity term is **[open]** for BT.
- **Label-free clustering metrics** (Xu, Lowe & Trappenberg, arXiv:2409.06612, 2024): k-means silhouette, clustering agreement, embedding entropy on unlabeled embeddings. Key failure: metrics correlate with linear-probe acc for **SimCLR & MoCo-v2 but NOT SimSiam** (entropy even *reverses* trend for SimSiam) → **proxy reliability is method-dependent**; untested on Barlow Twins. **[established that it's method-dependent]**
- **Held-out SSL loss**: SSL loss is a known-poor quality proxy (RankMe's motivation: "non-informative loss values"); a *held-out* loss removes the overfitting confound but still tracks the surrogate, not downstream. **[folklore]**

**Failure modes to flag:** RankMe known to (i) saturate above #classes, (ii) only rank within one method, (iii) be derived/validated at ImageNet scale on RN50 — **none of these papers test 1k-image pre-training**, so transfer to our tiny regime is **[open]**.

## Q3 — Early stopping in SSL with an in-domain signal

Genuinely thin. Standard practice is fixed-budget cosine, no early stop (Q1). Closest published in-domain stopping signals are the label-free monitors (arXiv:2409.06612 frames metrics as "how much longer to keep training") and RankMe-for-selection — but **no validated patience-based early-stopping recipe for SSL exists**, and none at 1k-image scale. Small-data SSL works (e.g. *Small is Beautiful*, arXiv:2103.13559; low-data survey S0925231224019702) advocate in-domain SSL but do **not** prescribe a stopping rule. **[open] — our kNN-patience stop is a contribution to defend, not a convention to cite.**

## Q4 — Practical design of an in-domain TI kNN probe (200 classes)

- **DINO protocol as the citable anchor**: k=20, cosine/dot-product weighting, τ=0.07, **full train set as db**, val as queries (Wu et al. 2018 instance-discrimination protocol, arXiv:1805.01978, originated k-NN-on-features with k≈200/full memory bank). **[established]**
- **Probe db source.** TI has an **official labeled val set (10k, 50/class, disjoint from train)**. Cleanest design: **db = a fixed held-out slice of TI train; queries = official TI val** (or split val 50/50). Do **not** use images that are in the pre-training subset as the db at the small splits (1k = 5/class) — at 1k the entire subset is too small to be both train data and a representative 200-class db, so the **official val set is the natural, leakage-free probe source**. **[established design principle]**
- **Class-coverage caveat at tiny scale.** 200 classes with k=20 and a small db is statistically noisy; published kNN evals use the *full* IN train (~1.3M) as db. With only ~10k TI val images (50/class) the kNN signal will be far noisier than any cited protocol → expect a jagged curve; smooth it before triggering anneal/stop. Consider reporting **kNN over the full TI val as db→val (leave-one-out)** to maximize db size. **[extrapolation — no paper validates kNN-monitor at this db size]**
- **Is using pre-training-domain *labels* for stopping acceptable?** Yes, and it is *more* standard than label-free: every method in Q1 that probes uses the pre-training set's own labels for the probe (DINO/DINOv2/MoCo-v3 kNN on IN). Using **TI labels** (the pre-training domain) for a stop signal is in-domain and standard; what was non-standard/biasing was using **CIFAR-10** (the eval domain). Fully label-free (RankMe) is *stricter* than the field requires but maximally defensible. **[established]**

## Q5 — Recommendation (ranked) for ViT-Tiny/8 @64px, TI subsets 1k–100k

1. **In-domain kNN on the official TI val set** (db = TI val or held-out TI-train slice, k=20, τ=0.07, DINO protocol). *Evidence:* strongest — directly mirrors DINO/DINOv2/MoCo-v3 in-domain practice (arXiv:2104.14294, 2304.07193). *Reliability at 1k:* moderate; signal is noisy with a ~10k/200-class db (no paper validates this db size) → smooth + use generous patience. *Cost:* one forward pass over ≤20k images every N steps — cheap. **Primary recommendation; the cross-domain bias is fully removed.**
2. **RankMe on 10–25k unlabeled TI embeddings** as a *label-free* corroborating signal / tie-breaker (arXiv:2210.02885). *Evidence:* established at IN scale, but saturates above #classes and untested at 1k images → **[open] at our scale**. *Cost:* one SVD of a 192-dim embedding matrix — trivial. Use **alongside** kNN, not as the sole trigger.
3. **Held-out TI SSL loss** as a cheap overfitting guard (compute BT loss on a fixed held-out TI slice). *Evidence:* folklore; known-weak downstream proxy. *Cost:* near-zero. Sanity check only, not a stopping criterion.

**Explicit grounding:** option 1's *protocol* (k, τ, db=train, query=val) is directly published (DINO `eval_knn.py`); using it *online for stopping* is our extrapolation. Option 2 is published for *model selection*, not online stopping; its small-data reliability is unverified. Do **not** claim large-scale RankMe/kNN correlations transfer to 1k-image pre-training — that is **[open]** and should be stated as a threat to validity. The defensible minimal change for the thesis: **swap the CIFAR-10 kNN for a TI-val kNN with identical patience logic**, and (optionally) log RankMe as a label-free cross-check.
